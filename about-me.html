Dayyanah is a certified Health Psychologist (HCPC reg.) with a wealth of experience in designing and delivering behaviour change interventions for patients who smoke or have a variety of health conditions (respiratory, oncology, pain).

She conducts multi-strand quantitative and qualitative research specialising in health and wellbeing. Her research skills include gaining ethics approval, designing study materials, collecting and analysing data (using SPSS and R), and disseminating her work through conference presentations and paper publications.

Dayyanah has in-depth experience in delivering undergraduate and postgraduate teaching sessions as well as delivering training to healthcare and non-healthcare professionals. She translates health and psychological research findings into insightful and innovative healthcare solutions. Dayyanah aspires to identify gaps and weaknesses in health services to offer insightful strategies to satisfy unmet needs.
